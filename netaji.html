Tribute to Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose

Early Life and Education

Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose was born on January 23, 1897 in Cuttack, Orissa. He was the ninth child of Janakinath Bose and Prabhavati Devi. Netaji studied at Ravenshaw Collegiate School and then went to Calcutta for higher studies. He obtained his Bachelor's degree from the University of Calcutta and then went to England to study law at the University of Cambridge.

What a great motivation Netaji is !!

Netaji infused courage and confidence in the hearts of freedom fighters. His army, named as Indian National Army (INA), was a startling example of Netaji’s superior warcraft and able leadership qualities. Some of his inspiring quotes he delivered to motivate the INA men in particular and the Indian citizens in general can throw more light on Netaji’s patriotism and sacrifice.

"One individual may die for an idea, but that idea will, after his death, incarnate itself in a thousand lives.""It is blood alone that can pay the price of freedom. Give me blood and I will give you freedom".

Freedom Fighter

Netaji was a prominent leader in the Indian independence movement against British rule. He founded the Forward Bloc in 1939 and later formed the Indian National Army to fight against the British during World War II. He was imprisoned by the British several times and went on hunger strikes to protest against the British rule.

Death

Netaji died on August 18, 1945 in a plane crash in Taipei, Taiwan. However, there are many conspiracy theories surrounding his death and some believe that he actually survived and went into hiding.
